max = 1 / (1 − f)

A kernel ≠ whole-program. If the part you speed up is fraction f of the runtime, the best the whole program can get is 1/(1−f) — no matter how fast the kernel becomes. Each gauge below fills toward that ceiling and is forbidden to cross it. A 700× kernel that is only 40% of runtime is DECLINED: its ceiling is 1.67×.
